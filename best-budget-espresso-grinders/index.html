Frequently asked questions

Can a grinder under $200 really dial in espresso?

Yes, a few can. The key is fine, repeatable adjustment in the espresso range. Some budget burr grinders are built or upgraded specifically for espresso and hold a setting well enough to pull good shots; many general filter grinders cannot go fine enough or repeatably enough.

Why does the grinder matter more than the espresso machine?

Because grind size and consistency control extraction. Espresso is unforgiving: a tiny change in fineness swings the shot from sour to bitter. A precise, repeatable grinder lets a modest machine shine, while a poor grinder bottlenecks even an expensive one.

What is grind retention and why should I care for espresso?

Retention is the coffee left inside the grinder after grinding. High retention means stale grounds carry into your next shot and weight-based dosing drifts. Single-dose grinders minimize it, which is why low-retention designs are popular even at the budget end.

Stepped or stepless adjustment for budget espresso?

Stepless lets you fine-tune in tiny increments, ideal for chasing a shot, but good stepped grinders with fine enough steps still dial in well. At this price, a clean, repeatable adjustment of either type matters more than the label.

Will a budget espresso grinder also handle filter coffee?

Often yes, with a wider adjustment range. A grinder that reaches fine espresso settings can usually open up for pour over and drip too, though a dedicated espresso-focused grinder may feel limited at the very coarse end. Check the model's stated range for both.
